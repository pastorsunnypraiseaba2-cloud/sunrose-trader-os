🛰️ Sunrose Market Intelligence
SUPPLEMENTARY

Combines Compression Score, Market Lifecycle, Trend Expectation, the Sunrose Trend Index, Overextension, Expansion Fuel, Extreme Price Alerts, and Live Market Analysis into a single decision dashboard. This is analytical context only -- it never triggers a trade on its own. A valid setup still requires the full Continuation or Reversal Strategy sequence from the Brain.

Sunrose Trend Index (STI)
One score (0-100) that summarizes everything else on this page -- width, compression, stretch, energy, and phase, combined. Higher = more of a real trend underway; lower = a quiet or dead market.
Market Lifecycle / Phase
Where the market is right now in its natural cycle: coiling up (Compression), breaking out (Expansion), stretched too far (Overextension), snapping back (Pullback), or settling before the next move (Re-accumulation).
Trend Expectation
A realistic read on how far this move could travel, given how the CPR is currently behaving -- not a guarantee, just an expectation based on similar past setups.
Extreme Price Alert
A warning specifically about the R4/S4 levels -- the outer edges of the expected range. Price reaching these levels has historically come with a higher chance of a pullback or reversal.
Current Position
Where price is sitting right now relative to the CPR levels (e.g. "inside the CPR" or "between R2 and R3").
Distance to Extreme
How far price still has to travel to reach R4 or S4 (or how far past it price already is).
Pullback Probability
The estimated odds of a reversal from exactly where price is right now. Low near the CPR center, higher the further price stretches out.
Overextension
A traffic-light read on whether price has moved too far, too fast: 🟢 Normal, 🟡 Extended (consider partial profits), 🔴 Overextended (a pullback is more likely).
Market Stretch vs. Market Energy vs. Expansion Fuel Remaining
These three often get confused because they sound similar -- but each answers a different question:
• Stretch = how far price has already moved from the CPR center, right now.
• Energy = how much force/momentum is behind the move -- is it pushing hard, or stalling?
• Fuel Remaining = how much room is left before price reaches the target, regardless of momentum.

A move can have low Energy but high Fuel Remaining at the same time -- that just means the move has stalled, but there's still plenty of untouched distance ahead if a fresh push comes. That's not a contradiction; think of a car stopped partway down a long, open road.
Sunrose Market Outlook
A forward-looking, next-24-hours style summary that pulls together everything above into one quick read: bias, expected behaviour, likely target, biggest risk, and the mindset to trade with.

📉 Backtest Engine
HISTORICAL REPLAY

Walks backward through ~7 months of hourly history for the current symbol, day by day, reconstructing exactly what the Brain would have seen at each point -- Monthly/Weekly/Daily CPR, the Market Structure Dashboard, liquidity sweeps, and structure -- with no look-ahead. Runs both the Continuation and Reversal Strategies independently on every day, using the same decision logic that drives your live Brain.

Uses your Deriv API token from Settings. Takes a few seconds -- fetches once, then walks the whole window in memory.

Structure and zones are auto-detected, not manually marked. Live trading lets you mark CHoCH/BOS/OB/FVG by eye. A historical replay can't ask you to do that for every day of 7 months, so it uses the app's existing swing-based auto-detection (the same engine behind "Auto-Detect" on the Structure/SMC tabs) at each point in time. This is generally reliable but will occasionally read structure differently than you would by eye.
Hourly resolution, not tick-by-tick. Trade outcomes (win/loss) are determined by walking forward through hourly candles. If a stop and target both fall inside the same hourly bar, the stop is assumed to hit first -- a conservative assumption so the backtest doesn't flatter itself.
One open trade per strategy at a time. Once a signal fires, the engine skips ahead past its resolution before looking for the next one in that strategy -- it won't stack multiple overlapping "trades" on the same still-valid setup.
Past performance on THIS specific window is not a guarantee. ~7 months of one symbol is a small, particular sample. Treat this as a sanity check on the logic, not proof the strategy will perform the same way going forward.
Tap "Run Backtest" above to walk the current symbol's history through both strategies.
